Key insights:
◆ wait() atomically unlocks mutex, sleeps thread, then relocks on wakeup - requires unique_lock's manual lock/unlock capability
◆ Always use predicate form cv.wait(lock, []{return ready;}) to automatically handle spurious wakeups and early notifications
◆ Condition variable doesn't protect shared state - the mutex does; always hold lock when modifying condition
◆ Notifying outside the lock is safe and often faster - woken thread acquires lock immediately instead of blocking again
◆ Use notify_one() when only one thread can proceed (single item added); notify_all() for broadcast events (shutdown)
◆ Include shutdown flag in wait predicates: cv.wait(lock, []{return shutdown || !queue.empty();}) for graceful termination
◆ Condition variables enable 0% CPU waiting (thread sleeps) vs 100% busy-waiting with 1-5μs wake latency
◆ Thundering herd problem: notify_all() with single-item queue wakes all consumers but only one proceeds - use notify_one()
